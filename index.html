What is Neurofeedback?

Neurofeedback is a form of biofeedback that uses real-time monitoring of brain activity to teach self-regulation. Using EEG sensors placed on the scalp, we measure your brainwave patterns and provide feedback through visual and auditory cues, helping your brain learn to function more efficiently.

How Does It Work?

Our process is simple, structured, and tailored to your specific needs. Here's what to expect:

1

Assessment

We review your history, symptoms, and run baseline measurements to understand your unique brain patterns.

2

Training

Non-invasive EEG sessions, typically 40-60 minutes, 1-3 times per week. You'll sit comfortably while your brain learns new patterns.

3

Progress

Most clients notice improvements within 5-10 sessions. We track your progress with regular assessments.

4

Results

Lasting changes typically achieved in 40-80 sessions. The improvements are durable because your brain has learned new, healthier patterns.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about neurofeedback therapy

EEG biofeedback was developed in the 1960s when researchers discovered that individuals could learn to alter their brainwave patterns through feedback. The field expanded significantly through military research and clinical studies. Over 50+ years of published research has explored its application for epilepsy, ADHD, addictions, anxiety, depression, PTSD, and many other conditions. Today it is used by clinics worldwide as a non-invasive approach to brain wellness.

No. The effects of neurofeedback are specific to electrode placement and frequency trained, meaning different training protocols produce different outcomes. Results are cumulative over sessions, exceed spontaneous recovery ranges, and can be reversed through different training protocols. None of these characteristics are consistent with a placebo effect. Research consistently demonstrates measurable changes in brain function following neurofeedback training.

We have worked with clients from age 5 to 70+. Many clinics around the world successfully work with children as young as 2-3 years old. Neurofeedback is non-invasive and gentle, making it suitable for a wide age range. The training approach is adapted to be age-appropriate and engaging for younger clients.

Changes typically begin to appear after 5-10 sessions. Most clients achieve lasting results in 40-80 sessions, with each session lasting 40-60 minutes, scheduled 1-3 times per week. The exact timeline depends on the condition being addressed, its severity, and individual response to training. We monitor progress throughout and adjust the treatment plan accordingly.

Biofeedback is the broader term referring to techniques that provide real-time feedback on physiological processes such as heart rate, muscle tension, skin temperature, and breathing. Neurofeedback is a specialized form of biofeedback that focuses specifically on brain function, using EEG monitoring to measure and train brainwave patterns. At our clinic, we use both approaches depending on your specific needs.

Many clients have been able to work with their physicians to reduce or eliminate medication as neurofeedback training takes effect. However, you should always consult your physician before making any changes to your medication. We are happy to communicate with your doctor about your progress to support informed decisions about your treatment plan.

Coverage varies by plan. Many extended health plans cover biofeedback under medical diagnostics or allied health services. Some plans may also cover associated services like counselling, acupuncture, or massage therapy. We recommend checking with your insurance provider, and we'll be happy to review your coverage during your intake appointment to help you understand your benefits.
